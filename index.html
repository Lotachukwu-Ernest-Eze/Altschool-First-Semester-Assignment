Profile

Lotachukwu Ernest Eze is a graduate of Chemical Engineering from the Federal University of Technology Owerri, with a deep passion for transforming education in Africa. He is dedicated to enhancing the quality of education across the continent, particularly through his one-on-one coaching in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) subjects, both online and offline. Over the years, Lotachukwu has taught and mentored over 70 students, both within Nigeria and internationally.
In addition to teaching, Lotachukwu is an accomplished writer and creator of educational videos, focused on simplifying complex concepts and making learning more accessible to students.
He served for three years as the leader of the Teens Solution Ministry in the Assemblies of God Enugu District, where he was devoted to nurturing teenagers to become representatives of God in all spheres of life.

Why AltSchool?

I joined AltSchool Africa to learn software engineering because I believe that technology is a crucial tool for making education more accessible. With the skills I acquire, I intend to develop innovative solutions that will support educational initiatives across Africa. My aim in the School of Engineering program is to gain the knowledge necessary to build and scale educational platforms that can reach diverse populations, particularly in underprivileged communities.
